James Frederic Michael
  • Dr. James Frederic Michael was born on 4 June 1941 in Lincoln, Lancaster County, Nebraska, United States.1
  • He was educated at University of Nebraska with a degree in dentistry before 1966.1
  • In 1966, James resided in Lebanon, Laclede County, Missouri, United States.1
  • He worked as a dentist until he retired in 1999 between 1966 and 1999 in Lebanon, Laclede County, Missouri, United States.1
  • He and Ann Lee Duvall were married on 2 September 1967 in Eureka, St. Louis County, Missouri, United States.1
  • James was listed as a surviving child of Jeanne Emilie Jahn in the obituary published on 1 September 2002 in the Lincoln Journal Star. "Michael - Jean Emilie, 94, Lincoln, widow of Frederick, died Friday (8/30/2002) of pulmonary fibrosis and has gone to be with Jesus. Beloved mother and grandmother. Born, Omaha (1/16/08) to James and Antoinette Jahn. Graduate, Omaha South High School; Nebraska Wesleyan University, University of Nebraska, Lincoln, Masters Degree. Attended, University of Omaha; University of California at Berkeley. Retired teachers. Taught seventh and 8th grade English, Omaha Public Schools. One of five Lincoln teachers city-wide chosen by Dr. Fredstrom to pilot the elementary gifted program in the 1950s. Taught fifth grade, and later upper elementary, gifted students at Hartley. Also taught at Pershing and Hawthorne elementary school, 1950s-1970s.
    "Jean and Fred belonged to several dance clubs in Lincoln and were also square dance callers. Jean was in charge of the children's dance festival, Pershing auditorium, 1950s. Member, Warren United Methodist Church; Lincoln Women's Chamber of Commerce, charter member. Jean was an avid reader and traveler. She has visited all 50 states, Europe, North Africa and most Canadian Provinces.
    "Survivors: daughter, Marilyn Michael, Lincoln; son, daughter-in-law, Dr. James and Ann, Lebanon, Mo.; grandsons, spouse, John Michael, Lebanon, Darrell and Sherri Michael; great-grandchildren, Mary Katelynn, Zachary Michael.
    "Private family services, Butherus, Maser and Love. Burial at Lincoln Memorial Park.".2
  • He died on 18 March 2008, at age 66, in Springfield, Greene County, Missouri, United States.1
  • He was buried on 21 March 2008 in Mount Rose Memorial Park, Lebanon, Laclede County, Missouri, United States.1

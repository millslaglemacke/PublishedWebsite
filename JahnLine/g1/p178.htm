Frank Jahn

Parents

  • Father: Adam M. Jahn (b. about 1853, d. 22 June 1907)
  • Mother: Caterina Maurek (b. about 1853, d. after 1900)
  • Frank Jahn was born about 1890 in Bohemia.1,2
  • He immigrated with his parents from Bremen on 5 Aug 1900 onboard the Koenigin Luise and arrived at Ellis Island, New York, United States, on 15 August 1900.3
  • He appeared on the 1920 census as an in-law to Fred James Ratay, Sr., in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States. Enumerated at 1334 Drexel Street were:
    1. Ratay, Fred, head, owns his house "free", age 40, works as a millwright for a packing house for wages.
    2. -----, Josie, wife, age 40.
    3. -----, Mary E., daughter, age 19, works as a forelady for a cracker company.
    4. -----, Joseph, son, age 18, works as an office clerk for a packing house.
    5. -----, Fred, son, age 16, attends school, works as an office clerk for a packing house.
    6. -----, James, son, 15, attends school.
    7. -----, Anna, daughter, age 13, attends school.
    8.-----, Helen, daughter, age 9, attends school.
    9. -----, Edward, son 4 1/12.
    10. Jahn, Frank, brother in law, age 29, works as a janitor for a "cost office." [note: it transcribed as cost house but I suspect it may be post office.]2
  • Frank Jahn died in 1944 in Fort Meade, Meade County, South Dakota, United States.

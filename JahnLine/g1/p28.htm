James John Jahn1

Parents

  • Father: Adam M. Jahn (b. about 1853, d. 22 June 1907)
  • Mother: Caterina Maurek (b. about 1853, d. after 1900)
  • James John Jahn was also known as Vaclav "James" J. Jahn von Neumark.2
  • He was born on 20 April 1883 in Domazlice, Bohemia.2,3,4,5,6,1
  • He immigrated with his parents from Bremen on 5 Aug 1900 onboard the Koenigin Luise and arrived at Ellis Island, New York, United States, on 15 August 1900.7
  • He and Antoinette Celicia Pliska were married on 9 October 1904 in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States.8,3,9
  • He appeared on the 1910 census on 20 April 1910 in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States. Enumerated at 478 22nd Street were:
    1. Jahn, James, age 26, married for 6 years, born Bohemia, immigrated in 1900, naturalized citizen, speaks English, baker.
    2. -----, Antoinette, age 23, married for 6 years, given birth to 2 children, born Bohemia, speaks English. [NOTE: I would expect this to say 3 children born and 2 living].
    3. -----, Jenny, age 2
    4. -----, Jimmy, age 4 or 9 months [the record is hard to decipher].3
  • James registered for the draft on 12 September 1918 in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States. James Jahn registered for the draft on 12 Sep 1918 in Omaha. His card gives that he resided at 4609 So. 10th, Omaha, Douglas, Nebr, age 24, born Apr 1884, white, naturalized citizen, occupation self-employed baker, employment address 4609 So. 10th, nearest relative is Antonetta Jahn who has the same residence. He was described as short, of medium build, with brown hair and brown eyes.5
  • He appeared on the 1920 census on 19 January 1920 in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States. Enumerated at 4609 20th Street South were:
    1. Jahn, James, age 35, married, immigrated in 1900, naturalized in 1917, owns a bakery.
    2. -----, Antoinette, age 33, married, unknown when immigrated, naturalized (no date given).
    3. -----, Jeanne, age 12, attends school.
    4. -----, James, age 9, attends school.
    5. -----, Edward, age 4, attends school.10
  • He appeared on the 1930 census on 10 April 1930 in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States. Enumerated at 4609 S. 20th Street:
    1. Jahn, James J., age 45, married, He owns his house which was valued at $2599 and owns a radio. He became a naturalized citizen in 1901. He owns a bakery and is not a veteran.
    2. -----, Antoinette, age 43, married, works at the bakery.
    3. -----, Jean E, age 22, works as a teacher in a public school.
    4. -----, Edward L., age 15, attends school.11
  • In 1935, James resided at Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States.12
  • In 1936, James resided at Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States. Two of their children, James F. and Edward L,. also live at this residence.132
  • He appeared on the 1940 census on 15 April 1940 in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States. Enumerated at 4609 S. 20th Street:
    1. Jahn, James, age 55, married, highest grade attended was 8th grade, unable to work, received income of $50 or more from sources other than money wages or salary.
    2. -----, Antoinette, age 52, highest grade attended was 8th grade.14
  • He worked as a Baker in 1941 in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States. He was a retired baker2,5
  • In May 1941, James resided at Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States. He had lived in the community for 41 years.2
  • He died on 9 May 1941, at age 58, in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States.2,1
  • He was buried on 12 May 1941 in Hill Crest, Omaha, NE, in Omaha, Douglas County, Nebraska, United States.2,1516
